Key takeaways
Ondo is the most complete pure-play in RWA tokenisation.
Four product lines, about $3.65B across the stack:
  • tokenised US Treasuries (OUSG, USDY)
  • a fast-growing tokenised-equities venue (Global Markets)
  • an institutional chain in testnet (Ondo Chain)
  • a new fund alongside State Street and Galaxy
The business and the token are two different investments.
Ondo's products generate real yield, but Ondo keeps almost none of it today and $ONDO captures none of it at all. The bull case for the token is largely a bet that the DAO eventually flips a fee switch, and that is optionality. It belongs at an optionality price, not a claim on cash flow.
The flagship product is a bet on interest rates, whether or not anyone says so.
USDY and OUSG holders earn roughly 3.5%, tracking the ~3.8% that short-term Treasuries now pay, less a thin spread Ondo keeps. That yield belongs to the US Treasury, not to Ondo, and it is already drifting lower as the Fed cuts.
The fight Ondo has to win is distribution, not technology.
BlackRock, Circle, and Franklin Templeton can tokenise a money-market fund any morning they decide to. Ondo's edge is being everywhere at once (multi-chain), staying neutral, and getting there first, rather than being technically untouchable.
The product stack

Ondo was founded in 2021 by Nathan Allman, out of Goldman Sachs's digital-assets team. Allman died unexpectedly in May 2026, and Ian De Bode, formerly president, is now chief executive. The company is chasing one thing: owning the rails for tokenised securities end to end, from the asset down to the settlement layer. Four product lines carry the story.

Permissioned validators, RWA-backed staking, enshrined oracles, native omnichain bridging. Ondo is onboarding broker-dealers and distributors before it opens the network, so the chain launches with real participants.

A new fund alongside State Street and Galaxy (the SWEEP fund) rounds out the stack, with Ondo as an anchor investor of about $200M.

Reading the numbers

Ondo is the second-largest platform in a tokenised-US-Treasury market that rwa.xyz puts at about $14.6B (mid-2026), inside a wider RWA market above $20B. The Global Markets volume figure is the traction signal I weight most, because trading volume is far harder to manufacture than a static TVL snapshot.

Two honesty notes on the data:

  • The treasury leaderboard is contested. Circle's USYC leads near $3.1B, Ondo follows around $2.7B, and Franklin Templeton's BENJI and BlackRock's BUIDL (issued through Securitize) sit just behind, so I would not hand Ondo a fixed market share. The honest read is a top-three platform in treasuries, second at the moment but losing ground recently as OUSG bleeds even while USDY holds, and a clear leader in tokenised equities.
  • Headline TVL is a directional read, not gospel. In 2025 an investor publicly questioned whether some RWA TVL is overstated. That was an allegation, not a proven finding, but it is a fair reminder to account for redemption mechanics and double-counting across wrapped products.
The central thesis: platform versus token

A lot of coverage answers two separate questions as if they were one. Keeping them apart is the whole point of this note.

the platform
Generates yield, keeps little.
  • A large, sticky asset base of about $3.65B.
  • OUSG's management fee waived to January 2027.
  • USDY's yield passed through to holders bar a thin spread.
A strong franchise whose monetisation is mostly switched off today, by choice, to win share.
fee-switch optionality
(the DAO has not voted)
the token ($ONDO)
Captures none of it today.
  • Governance, and not much else.
  • No contractual claim on the company's earnings.
  • Its price is the market pre-paying for a future the DAO has not voted on.
Closer to a share that pays no dividend: a value case more contingent than the platform's.
Sizing the gap (back of the envelope)

The token is worth about $1.5B today and about $3.1B fully diluted. The products generate on the order of $55M a year of yield. Ondo keeps almost none of it today: on the public trackers its current protocol revenue rounds to zero, and the most it has ever booked is about $21M, recorded before the fees were switched off. The token receives none of it. So roughly $3.1B of fully diluted value rests on optionality rather than on cash flow. The assumptions are explicit and the figures move; the point is the shape, not false precision.

The discipline is to price the fee switch as optionality, rather than quietly treat platform growth as if it already flows to the token.

The entire pitch of USDY and OUSG is T-bill yield, on-chain, around the clock. That yield belongs to the US Treasury, not to Ondo, and it leaves when rates do. In fact, it has already started: short-term Treasury yields have fallen from about 4.3% a year ago to about 3.8% now as the Fed cuts, and holders, who receive that yield less a thin spread Ondo keeps, are down to about 3.5%. If the cutting cycle continues, tokenised treasuries look less compelling next to other on-chain yield.

I spend my day job pricing cost of capital and rate-sensitive cash flows, so this is the risk I flag first, and the one the standard bull case tends to pass over.

The bull case survives only if:

  • rates stabilise; or
  • Ondo scales equities and Ondo Chain fast enough to outgrow the yield it is losing.

A meaningful share of $ONDO also trades in perpetual-futures markets, which tends to amplify the moves around the annual unlock dates. Ondo's own team wallet has also moved large $ONDO tranches to exchanges through 2026, on the order of $100M across several months, with no public explanation. A deposit is not a sale, so I treat this as a supply overhang to watch rather than confirmed insider selling. A re-rating that finally connects token to platform is the bull thesis; the unlock calendar is the clock ticking against it.

Risk and bear case: what would falsify the thesis
  1. A sustained cutting cycle erodes the core yield proposition before equities and Ondo Chain are big enough to cover the gap.
    Watch for: USDY and OUSG growth stalling as the policy rate falls.
  2. If the DAO never flips the fee switch, or governance value never really shows up, a thriving platform and a stranded token can coexist indefinitely.
    Watch for: a fee-switch proposal failing or being parked.
  3. BlackRock, Circle, and Franklin Templeton bring brand, trust, and balance sheet. If tokenised treasuries become a feature the giants offer rather than a category Ondo leads, Ondo's treasury edge thins to the equities and infrastructure layers.
    Watch for: Ondo's treasury share on rwa.xyz drifting down as BUIDL, USYC, and BENJI scale.
  4. Tokenised securities are securities. The SEC-registration route is a bet that the compliant path is the durable one. An adverse ruling, or a faster competitor on that same path, redraws the map.
    Watch for: the outcome of the Global Markets registration.
  5. Ondo's founder and chief executive died unexpectedly in May 2026, and a new chief executive took over mid-flight, with the SEC registration pending and Ondo Chain still pre-mainnet. A transition like this can unsettle a founder-led company at exactly the wrong moment.
    Watch for: senior departures, a shift in strategy, or slippage in the SEC and mainnet timelines.
  6. Ondo Chain mainnet slipping, or institutional partners not arriving with real liquidity, would undercut the end-to-end-rails thesis. Headline TVL should always be checked against rwa.xyz rather than taken at face value.
    Watch for: mainnet timing and the calibre of institutions that actually show up.
What I would monitor

In priority order, because that order is the thesis.

  1. 01Any fee-switch governance proposal and how it is framed, because that is the catalyst that closes the platform-token gap.
  2. 02The policy-rate path, and what it does to USDY and OUSG growth.
  3. 03The leadership transition under the new chief executive, and whether the SEC and mainnet timelines hold.
  4. 04Ondo Chain mainnet timing, and the calibre of institutions that actually show up.
  5. 05Ondo's treasury share on rwa.xyz against BUIDL, USYC, and BENJI.
  6. 06The January unlocks, and how the market positions into them.
Bottom line

Ondo is the best-positioned operator in a category that is real and growing, with a franchise that is genuinely strong and a token whose value case is more contingent than the platform's. The honest way to hold a view is to underwrite the business and the token separately. Price the fee switch as the optionality it still is, and do not assume today's yields are permanent.
